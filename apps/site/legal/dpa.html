Data Processing Agreement

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between Orbicheck (the "Processor") and you (the "Controller") and governs the processing of personal data. It ensures compliance with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR. Replace with your legal counsel–approved DPA.

1. Definitions

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by the Processor on behalf of the Controller, including customer emails, addresses, phone numbers, and tax IDs.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, validation, normalization, deduplication, and storage.

"Data Subject" means the individual to whom the Personal Data relates.

2. Scope and Purpose

The Processor processes Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing data validation, deduplication, and risk assessment services to the Controller, as outlined in the Terms of Service. Processing includes validation against public databases (e.g., VIES for EU VAT, government tax ID registries), normalization, and enrichment for operational efficiency.

3. Roles and Responsibilities

The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing. The Processor acts as a data processor and shall not process Personal Data for any other purpose without the Controller's written consent. The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data protection.

4. Data Subject Rights

The Processor shall assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests under applicable laws, including access, rectification, erasure, and restriction of processing. The Controller is responsible for handling such requests directly.

5. Security Measures

The Processor implements industry-standard security measures, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, regular audits, and incident response plans. Details are available upon request and subject to NDA.

6. Data Breaches

In the event of a data breach, the Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay, but no later than 72 hours, providing details of the breach, affected data, and mitigation steps.

7. Subprocessors

The Processor may engage subprocessors (e.g., cloud providers like AWS or database services). A current list is available at orbicheck.io/subprocessors. The Processor shall ensure subprocessors provide sufficient guarantees for data protection.

8. International Data Transfers

Personal Data may be transferred to countries outside the EEA. The Processor ensures transfers comply with GDPR (e.g., via Standard Contractual Clauses) and offers data residency options for Enterprise plans.

9. Retention and Deletion

Personal Data is retained only as necessary for the service (typically 7–365 days per plan). Upon termination or request, the Processor shall delete or return data securely, subject to legal retention requirements.

10. Audits and Inspections

The Controller may audit the Processor's compliance annually, subject to reasonable notice and NDA. The Processor shall provide audit reports or certifications upon request.

11. Termination

This DPA terminates upon termination of the Terms of Service. Obligations regarding data protection survive termination.

12. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction, e.g., Ireland] and subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts therein.

13. Amendments

Changes to this DPA will be communicated with reasonable notice. Continued use constitutes acceptance.
