Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

This Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) forms part of and is subject to the Orbitcheck Terms of Service or other written agreement governing your use of the Services (the “Agreement”) between Orbitcheck (“Processor”) and the customer entity identified in the Agreement (“Controller”). This DPA applies to Processor’s Processing of Personal Data on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services. In case of conflict between this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA prevails to the extent of the conflict. Capitalized terms not defined in this DPA have the meanings in the Agreement.

If you require a countersigned copy of this DPA, contact dpa@orbitcheck.io.

1. Definitions

“Data Protection Laws” means all laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data under the Agreement, including as applicable: the EU GDPR, the UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018, the Swiss FADP, and U.S. state privacy laws such as the CCPA/CPRA (collectively, “Applicable Laws”).

“EU GDPR” means Regulation (EU) 2016/679; “UK GDPR” has the meaning in the UK Data Protection Act 2018; “FADP” means the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection; “CCPA/CPRA” means California Civil Code §1798.100 et seq., as amended.

“Personal Data” means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that Processor Processes on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services.

“Processing” means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, validation, normalization, deduplication, enrichment, storage, caching, retrieval, disclosure, or deletion.

“Services” means Orbitcheck’s data validation, deduplication, entity resolution, and risk-driven order control services and related APIs, SDKs, connectors, dashboards, and support.

“Subprocessor” means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

“SCCs” means the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries (Module Two: Controller to Processor), and any UK and Swiss addenda or equivalents, each as updated or replaced from time to time.

“Security Incident” means a breach of Processor’s security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data transmitted, stored or otherwise Processed by Processor.

2. Scope, Instructions, and Purpose

Processor will Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including as necessary to provide, maintain, secure, and improve the Services, to comply with law, and as otherwise set out in this DPA and the Agreement. The subject-matter, nature, purpose, duration, categories of Personal Data, and Data Subjects are described in Annex I. Processor will notify Controller if, in Processor’s opinion, an instruction infringes Applicable Laws (to the extent legally permitted).

3. Roles and Responsibilities

Controller determines the purposes and means of Processing. Controller is responsible for (a) ensuring it has a lawful basis and appropriate transparency notices for the Processing; (b) providing accurate, relevant, and minimized data; and (c) not submitting Prohibited Data (see Section 14). Processor acts as a processor/service provider/contractor and will not “sell” or “share” Personal Data (as those terms are defined by the CCPA/CPRA) or otherwise Process Personal Data for advertising, profiling, or purposes other than providing the Services, except as permitted by Applicable Laws or this DPA.

4. Confidentiality and Personnel

Processor ensures that persons authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations and receive appropriate privacy and security training. Processor will limit access to Personal Data to personnel who need such access to provide the Services.

5. Security Measures

Processor implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, taking into account the nature of Processing and the risks involved. These measures include encryption in transit and at rest, access controls and least privilege, multi-factor authentication for administrative access, network and application security, logging and monitoring, vulnerability and patch management, secure software development practices, business continuity and disaster recovery, and vendor risk management, as further described in Annex II. Processor may update security measures from time to time provided the overall level of protection is not materially diminished.

6. Security Incidents

Processor will notify Controller without undue delay (and in any case within 72 hours after confirmation) upon becoming aware of a Security Incident affecting Personal Data, and will provide information reasonably available about the nature of the incident, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned, likely consequences, and measures taken or proposed to address the incident. Processor will take reasonable steps to mitigate adverse effects and will cooperate with Controller’s reasonable requests, taking into account Processor’s obligations to law enforcement and confidentiality.

7. Data Subject Requests and Assistance

Taking into account the nature of the Processing, Processor will assist Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, to respond to requests to exercise rights under Applicable Laws (e.g., access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). If Processor receives a request directly from a Data Subject, Processor will, unless prohibited by law, direct the Data Subject to submit the request to Controller.

8. DPIAs and Regulatory Cooperation

Processor will provide reasonable assistance to Controller with data protection impact assessments, prior consultations with supervisory authorities, and security assessments required by Applicable Laws, in each case solely in relation to the Processing of Personal Data by Processor and taking into account the nature of the Processing and information available to Processor.

9. Subprocessors

Controller authorizes Processor to engage Subprocessors to support the Services. A current list is available at orbitcheck.io/subprocessors (“Subprocessor List”). Processor will impose data protection obligations on Subprocessors that are no less protective than those in this DPA and remains responsible for Subprocessors’ performance. Processor will provide notice of new Subprocessors by updating the Subprocessor List and, where legally required, via email subscription. Controller may object to a new Subprocessor on reasonable data protection grounds within 15 days of notice; if unresolved, Controller may terminate the affected Services without penalty as its sole and exclusive remedy.

10. International Data Transfers

Processor may Process Personal Data globally, including outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland, subject to appropriate transfer mechanisms. Where Personal Data is transferred to a third country that does not ensure an adequate level of protection, the parties agree that the SCCs (Module Two: Controller to Processor), as incorporated by reference and completed by Annex I and Annex II of this DPA, will apply, together with the UK International Data Transfer Addendum and Swiss addendum as applicable. For clarity, the SCCs and applicable addenda prevail in the event of conflict with this DPA. Data residency options may be available for eligible plans.

11. Retention, Return, and Deletion

Processor retains Personal Data only for as long as necessary to provide the Services, to comply with the Agreement and Applicable Laws, and as described in Annex I (e.g., by plan configuration, typically between 7 and 365 days). Upon termination or upon Controller’s written request, Processor will delete or return Personal Data (at Controller’s election) and delete existing copies within a reasonable period, unless retention is required by law. Deletion of data in backups will occur in the ordinary course of backup rotation.

12. Audits and Information

Processor will make available to Controller information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and, upon reasonable advance notice no more than once per year (unless required by a supervisory authority or following a Security Incident), allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by Controller or an independent auditor mandated by Controller, subject to confidentiality and security requirements. Processor may satisfy audit requests by providing third-party audit reports, certifications, or summaries of penetration tests, where available.

13. Government and Third-Party Requests

If a government, law enforcement, or third party requests access to Personal Data, Processor will (i) notify Controller promptly unless legally prohibited, (ii) use commercially reasonable efforts to redirect the request to Controller, and (iii) disclose only the minimum necessary to comply with the request after assessing its legality.

14. California (CCPA/CPRA) Terms

To the extent the CCPA/CPRA applies, Processor acts as Controller’s “service provider” and/or “contractor.” Processor will not: (a) sell or share Personal Information; (b) retain, use, or disclose Personal Information for any purpose other than the business purposes specified in the Agreement, this DPA, or as permitted by the CCPA/CPRA; (c) combine Personal Information with personal information that Processor receives from another source, except as permitted by the CCPA/CPRA to provide the Services. Processor certifies that it understands and will comply with these obligations and will enable Controller to comply with consumer requests.

15. Prohibited Data and Controller Responsibilities

Unless expressly agreed in writing, Controller will not submit and Processor has no obligation to Process: (i) special categories of personal data under GDPR (e.g., health, biometric, genetic, racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, sexual orientation), (ii) precise geolocation, (iii) payment card primary account numbers, full bank account numbers, or credentials, (iv) government identification document scans or images, or (v) children’s data under the age of 16. For clarity, the Services support validation of business and tax identifiers expressly listed in Annex I (e.g., EU VAT IDs, ES NIF/NIE, BR CPF/CNPJ, MX RFC, AR CUIT, CL RUT, PE RUC/DNI) for legitimate business purposes determined by Controller. Controller is solely responsible for its compliance with Applicable Laws, including obtaining any required consents or notices and ensuring a lawful basis.

16. De-identified and Aggregated Data

Processor may create and use de-identified and/or aggregated data for purposes such as analytics, benchmarking, and improving the Services, provided such data does not identify or reasonably relate to a specific Data Subject or Controller and Processor implements technical safeguards and public commitments consistent with Applicable Laws to prevent re-identification.

17. Liability; Order of Precedence

Each party’s liability arising from or related to this DPA is subject to the limitations and exclusions of liability in the Agreement; provided that nothing limits either party’s liability to the extent such limitation is prohibited by Applicable Laws or the SCCs. In the event of conflict between: (i) this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA controls; (ii) this DPA and the SCCs, the SCCs control.

18. Term, Governing Law, and Amendments

This DPA is effective for the term of the Agreement and will automatically terminate upon termination of the Agreement, except for provisions that by their nature survive. Governing law and venue are as set forth in the Agreement; where the SCCs apply, the SCCs’ governing law provisions control. Processor may update this DPA as required to reflect changes in law or the Services; material changes will be communicated with reasonable notice. Continued use of the Services after the effective date of changes constitutes acceptance.

Annex I — Details of Processing

Subject Matter: Processing Personal Data submitted by Controller via API, connectors, and dashboard to provide data validation, deduplication, entity resolution, and risk-driven order controls.

Nature and Purpose: Validation against public or semi-public registries and datasets (e.g., EU VIES for VAT validation; ES NIF/NIE; BR CPF/CNPJ; MX RFC; AR CUIT; CL RUT; PE RUC/DNI), normalization, deduplication, fuzzy matching (e.g., similarity scoring), enrichment for operational efficiency, fraud- and risk-related heuristics (e.g., PO Box controls, COD/RTO checks), and generation of structured results and audit logs for operational use.

Duration: For the term of the Agreement and in accordance with retention settings selected by Controller or applicable plan defaults (typically 7–365 days), unless otherwise required by law.

Categories of Data Subjects: Customers and prospective customers of Controller (consumers and business contacts), Controller’s personnel who use the Services, and other individuals whose data Controller submits.

Categories of Personal Data (as determined by Controller and depending on features used):

  • Identification and contact: full name, email address, phone number.
  • Addresses: shipping/billing address lines, city, region, postal code, country.
  • Official and tax identifiers for validation: EU VAT ID, ES NIF/NIE, BR CPF/CNPJ, MX RFC, AR CUIT, CL RUT, PE RUC/DNI, and similar supported IDs as documented.
  • Order and account metadata: order IDs, external customer IDs, timestamps, structured reason codes and risk signals produced by the Services.
  • Technical data: IP addresses, user agent/device information, API credentials identifiers, event logs necessary for security and troubleshooting.
