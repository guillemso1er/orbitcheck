Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Orbitcheck ("Orbitcheck," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects Personal Data when you visit our websites, use our APIs, SDKs, connectors, and dashboards (the "Services"), or otherwise interact with us. It also describes your privacy rights and how to exercise them.

If you require a countersigned copy or have questions, contact privacy@orbitcheck.io.

Highlights:

  • We process end-customer data on your instructions to validate addresses, phones, emails, and business/tax IDs.
  • We do not sell or share Personal Data for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Data retention is configurable by plan (typically 7–365 days); caches and logs are short-lived.
  • Strong security: encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, monitoring, and incident response.

1) Who We Are and Scope

Orbitcheck provides API-first data hygiene and validation for ecommerce: addresses, phones, emails, EU VAT, and ES–LATAM tax IDs with deduplication, entity resolution, and risk-driven order controls.

This Policy applies to: (a) visitors to our websites and people who interact with our marketing or sales (we act as a controller); and (b) customers and their end users whose Personal Data is submitted to the Services (we generally act as a processor/service provider on behalf of our customers). For processor activities, our Data Processing Addendum governs.

2) Roles; End-User Data

For data you send to the Services about your customers, leads, or orders, you are the controller (or business) and Orbitcheck is your processor (or service provider/contractor). We process such data only on your documented instructions. If you are an end user of a merchant using Orbitcheck, please contact that merchant to exercise your privacy rights.

3) Personal Data We Collect

The Personal Data we process depends on how you interact with us and which features you use.

  • Account and Contact Data: name, company, role, email, phone, login identifiers, workspace/user IDs.
  • Service (Customer) Data: data you submit to validate or deduplicate, such as names, emails, phone numbers, shipping/billing addresses, and supported identifiers (e.g., EU VAT IDs, ES NIF/NIE, BR CPF/CNPJ, MX RFC, AR CUIT, CL RUT, PE RUC/DNI). We also generate structured reason codes, match scores, and risk signals.
  • Transaction and Billing Data: subscription tier, invoices, tax/VAT numbers, and payment status. Card data is processed by our payment processor; we do not store full card numbers.
  • Technical and Usage Data: IP address, device/browser/user agent, API keys/IDs, request metadata, logs, diagnostics, and event telemetry needed to secure and operate the Services.
  • Support Content: information you send to support, including attachments or screenshots.
  • Public/Third-Party Sources: validation against public or semi-public registries (e.g., EU VIES, government tax ID registries) and data from service providers acting on our behalf.

We do not seek to collect special categories of data (e.g., health, biometric, or precise geolocation), images of identity documents, full bank details, or children's data. Please do not send such data to the Services unless we have expressly agreed in writing.

4) How We Use Personal Data

  • Provide, maintain, validate, deduplicate, and improve the Services (including caching for performance and entity resolution).
  • Secure the Services, prevent abuse and fraud, and ensure integrity and availability.
  • Operate account management, billing, and customer support.
  • Develop new features, perform troubleshooting, analytics, and quality assurance using de-identified or aggregated data where possible.
  • Send service and transactional communications; send product updates or marketing where permitted (you can opt out).
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce terms.

5) Legal Bases (EU/UK/Swiss)

Where GDPR/UK GDPR/Swiss FADP applies, we rely on: (a) contractual necessity to provide the Services; (b) legitimate interests (e.g., security, fraud prevention, product improvement, and communicating with business users); (c) consent where required (e.g., certain cookies/marketing); and (d) legal obligations.

6) How We Share Personal Data

  • Subprocessors/Service Providers that host, store, or process data for us (e.g., cloud infrastructure, databases, analytics, email). Our current list is at orbitcheck.io/subprocessors.
  • Integrations You Authorize (e.g., Shopify/Woo/BigCommerce/Magento connectors) to the extent needed to deliver the integration.
  • Professional Advisors (legal, auditors) under confidentiality.
  • Legal/Compliance when required by law, to protect rights and safety, or to respond to lawful requests.
  • Corporate Transactions (merger, acquisition, financing, or sale) subject to appropriate safeguards and notice as required.
  • De-identified/Aggregated Data for analytics and improvement that does not identify individuals or customers.

We do not sell or share Personal Data for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined by the CCPA/CPRA, and we do not use sensitive Personal Data to infer characteristics about individuals.

7) International Data Transfers

We may transfer Personal Data to countries where we or our subprocessors operate, including outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland. When we do, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, and we implement additional measures as needed. Data residency options may be available for eligible plans.

8) Retention

We retain Personal Data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Policy and our contract with you. For Service (Customer) Data, retention is configurable by plan or by your settings (typically 7–365 days). Ephemeral caches and most logs are short-lived and purged in the ordinary course; backups are deleted on a rolling schedule. We may retain limited data to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

9) Security

We implement technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls and least privilege, MFA for administrative access, network and application security, logging and monitoring, vulnerability management, business continuity and disaster recovery, and vendor risk management. For more details, see our Security Measures.

10) Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to request: access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, and to object to certain processing. Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw consent at any time. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

California and other U.S. states (e.g., CO, CT, VA, UT) provide additional rights: to know, delete, correct, and to opt out of sale/share or targeted advertising. We do not sell or share Personal Data for cross-context behavioral advertising. We honor browser-based Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where legally required.

To exercise your rights, contact privacy@orbitcheck.io. If you are an end user of a merchant using Orbitcheck, please direct your request to that merchant (the controller). We may need to verify your identity and will respond within the timeframe required by law.

11) Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate and secure the Services, remember preferences, and analyze usage. Where required, we request your consent for non-essential cookies. You can manage preferences via your browser settings and, if available, our cookie banner. For details, see our Cookie Notice.

12) Children's Privacy

Our Services are not directed to children and we do not knowingly collect Personal Data from individuals under 16. If you believe a child has provided us Personal Data, contact us and we will delete it.

13) Data Minimization; Prohibited Data

Send only data necessary for validation and operational use. Unless expressly agreed in writing, do not submit special categories of data, precise geolocation, payment card primary account numbers, full bank account numbers, identity document images, or children's data. While certain tax identifiers may be considered sensitive under some laws, we use them solely to provide the Services and not to infer characteristics.

14) Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy to reflect changes to our practices, legal requirements, or the Services. If we make material changes, we will provide reasonable notice (e.g., in-app notice or email). The "Last updated" date below indicates when this Policy was last revised.

15) Contact Us

Questions, requests, or complaints:
Email: privacy@orbitcheck.io
Security incidents: security@orbitcheck.io
